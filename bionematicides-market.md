<p><p>The bionematicides market primarily consists of two types: microbials and biochemical. Microbial bionematicides utilize living organisms, such as bacteria, fungi, or nematodes, to target and control nematode populations in soil or plants, promoting healthier crops. In contrast, biochemical bionematicides are derived from natural substances, including plant extracts and secondary metabolites, aiming to disrupt nematode activity or enhance plant defenses. Both types offer eco-friendly alternatives to chemical nematicides, supporting sustainable agricultural practices and reducing environmental impact.</p></p>

<p><p>The Diammonium Phosphate (DAP) fertilizer market is primarily divided into two types: crystalline and granular. Crystalline DAP is typically produced through a process that yields fine, uniform particles, promoting quick nutrient absorption and efficient plant uptake. Conversely, granular DAP is coarser and more robust, ideal for bulk application and soil integration, providing a longer-lasting nutrient release. Both forms serve diverse agricultural needs, with choice depending on factors such as soil conditions, crop type, and application methods.</p></p>

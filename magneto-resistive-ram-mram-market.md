<p><p>Magneto Resistive RAM (MRAM) is a non-volatile memory technology that utilizes magnetic states to store data, offering high performance and endurance. The MRAM market is primarily divided into two types: Toggle MRAM and Spin-Transfer Torque MRAM (STT-MRAM). Toggle MRAM operates through magnetic toggling of bits, providing fast write speeds but with larger cell sizes. Conversely, STT-MRAM leverages spin-polarized currents to switch magnetic states, enabling higher density and lower power consumption, making it suitable for advanced applications in computing and data storage.</p></p>

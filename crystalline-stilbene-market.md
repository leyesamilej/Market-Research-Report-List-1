<p><p>The crystalline stilbene market is primarily categorized into two types: (E)-stilbene and (Z)-stilbene. (E)-stilbene, also known as trans-stilbene, features a more stable structure and is commonly used in organic synthesis, optical applications, and as a fluorescence marker. In contrast, (Z)-stilbene, or cis-stilbene, possesses a distinct geometry that affects its chemical properties and potential applications. Both types play significant roles in research and industry, influencing various sectors such as pharmaceuticals, materials science, and biotechnology.</p></p>

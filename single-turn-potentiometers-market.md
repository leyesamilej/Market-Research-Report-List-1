<p><p>The single-turn potentiometer market is segmented into non-contact and contact types. Non-contact single-turn potentiometers utilize magnetic or optical methods to measure resistance without physical wear, offering high reliability and longevity. In contrast, contact single-turn potentiometers rely on direct electrical contact with a resistive element, providing accurate readings but may experience wear over time. Both types find applications in various industries, including automotive, consumer electronics, and automation, catering to diverse needs for precision and durability.</p></p>
